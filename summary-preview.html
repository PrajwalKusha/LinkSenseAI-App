I Gave 2 Hours Daily to DSA & System Design, Best Decision Ever

1
Motivation for Starting the Challenge
The author, a backend developer with 1.5 years of experience, felt inadequate in interviews, meetings, and daily tasks, such as explaining APIs or scalable designs, leading to faked confidence and secret Googling; to overcome this, he committed to 2 hours daily for 30 days focused on DSA & System Design, emphasizing that consistency trumps courses or luck for skill-building.
2
Simple Daily Commitment Rule
Adopt a no-excuses routine of 1 hour on DSA (e.g., solving LeetCode problems) and 1 hour on System Design (e.g., learning concepts like rate limiting), without rigid targets or pressure, prioritizing habit formation through daily showing up to foster gradual improvement.
3
Week 1 Struggles and Habit Building
Initial sessions were tough, with easy DSA problems taking 40 minutes and System Design concepts like Token Bucket feeling overwhelming, but persisting by noting partial understandings (even 20%) in personal words helped establish the habit, reminding that the goal is growth, not perfection.
4
Week 2 Small Wins and Pattern Recognition
By day 10, daily practice led to quicker problem-solving (e.g., prefix sums in 20 minutes) and recognizing patterns like sliding windows in DSA, while grasping System Design ideas like message queues for async processing (e.g., Kafka in payment systems), shifting mindset from "I've seen this" to "I understand this" for a confidence boost.
5
Week 3 Ritual Formation and Enjoyment
The practice evolved into a rewarding ritual, becoming the favorite part of the day with focused sessions on topics like gRPC vs. REST or designing systems for apps like WhatsApp, free from distractions, reigniting the joy of learning without external motivation.
